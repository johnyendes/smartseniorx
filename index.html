Estate & Life Planning

Organize Everything. Protect Everyone.

The most overlooked part of retirement — making sure your family can find everything when it matters most. Documents, accounts, passwords, wishes.

📋
Emergency Life Binder
Identity records, financial accounts, insurance policies, property, medical info, and key contacts — all organized so your family finds everything instantly.
🔐
Digital Asset Registry
Bank logins, brokerage accounts, crypto wallets, PayPal, social media, domain names. Modern estates are 30–40% digital — most families never find them.
⚖️
Will vs. Trust Explainer
Understand the difference. Wills go through probate (months or years). A Revocable Living Trust transfers assets immediately and privately — very common for retirees.
🏥
Healthcare Directives
Living will, healthcare power of attorney, and DNR instructions. Without these, hospitals and courts decide — not your family.
💼
Durable Power of Attorney
Authorizes someone to manage your finances if you become incapacitated. Without it, families often need expensive court guardianship proceedings.
⚰️
Funeral & Final Wishes
Burial or cremation preferences, service wishes, obituary notes, prepaid arrangements, and final messages to family. Often the most impactful thing you can leave.

🏥 Healthcare
Portugal has a strong public healthcare system (SNS). Legal residents can access it at low cost. Private expat insurance runs €80–€200/month. Lisbon and Porto have excellent private hospitals. No Medicare coverage abroad — private insurance is essential.
Healthcare Quality: A
📋 D7 Visa Requirements
The D7 "Passive Income Visa" requires proof of ~€820/month minimum passive income (SS, pension, or investment). Apply at the Portuguese consulate before arrival. Renew after 2 years; permanent residency after 5. Processing time: 2–4 months.
Retiree-Friendly Visa: Yes
💰 Taxes
Portugal has a US-Portugal tax treaty. US Social Security is taxed only in the US. The NHR (Non-Habitual Resident) tax regime offered 10% flat tax on foreign pensions — it was replaced in 2024 by IFICI, which still offers reduced rates for qualifying retirees.
Tax Treaty with US: Yes
🏦 Banking
Open a Portuguese bank account (Millennium BCP, Novo Banco) after arriving. Use Wise for transfers — typically saves 3–5% vs bank wire. FATCA applies: you'll file FinCEN 114 (FBAR) if foreign account balances exceed $10,000.

🏥 Healthcare
Private healthcare in Mexico is excellent and costs 60–80% less than the US. Top facilities in Mexico City, Guadalajara, and San Miguel de Allende. IMSS (public insurance) available to legal residents for ~$400/year. Most expats use private insurance ($100–$200/mo) or pay out-of-pocket.
Cost Savings: 60–80% vs US
📋 Visa Requirements
Temporary Residency requires $1,620/mo income (SS + pension counts). Permanent Residency (4 years) requires ~$2,700/mo. Apply at Mexican consulate before entry. Renew annually for first 4 years. Extremely common for US retirees — most applications succeed easily.
Most Accessible Visa: Yes
💰 Taxes
US-Mexico tax treaty exists. As a US citizen you still file US taxes regardless of where you live. Mexico taxes residents on Mexico-sourced income. Social Security income is generally taxed only in the US under the treaty. Consult a US expat tax specialist.
Tax Treaty: Yes
🌅 Top Expat Cities
San Miguel de Allende (colonial, large expat community), Lake Chapala / Ajijic (largest US expat concentration in the world), Mérida (affordable, Yucatan), Puerto Vallarta (beach, active community), Mexico City (urban, world-class amenities).

📋 Pensionado Visa
The Pensionado (retiree) visa is among the best in the world. Requires just $1,000/mo from a permanent income source like Social Security or a pension. Provides major import duty discounts, property tax discounts, and airline discounts. No minimum stay required. Renewable indefinitely.
Best Retiree Visa in Region
🏥 Healthcare
CAJA public healthcare is available to legal residents for $70–$150/month. Private healthcare costs 50–70% less than the US. CIMA Hospital in San José is internationally accredited. Many retirees use both: CAJA for routine care, private for specialist access.
CAJA Public Health: Available
💰 Taxes
No US-Costa Rica tax treaty. Costa Rica uses a territorial tax system — income earned outside Costa Rica is not taxed there. You still file US taxes. Foreign income brought into Costa Rica is not taxed. US Social Security remains taxed in the US.
Territorial Tax: Favorable

📋 Jubilado Visa — Best in the World
Panama's Jubilado (retiree) visa is widely considered the most generous retirement visa globally. Requires $1,000/mo from a government pension source. Perks include: 50% off movies/sports events, 30% off buses/trains, 25% off flights, 20% off medical care, 15% off hospitals, and more.
Best Retiree Benefits: Global
💰 Tax Advantage
Panama uses territorial taxation — foreign-sourced income (including US Social Security and pensions) is not taxed in Panama. Combined with the USD currency (no exchange risk), Panama is the most financially straightforward expat destination for US retirees.
Foreign Income: Untaxed
🏥 Healthcare
Panama City has some of Latin America's best private hospitals (Hospital Punta Pacífica, affiliated with Johns Hopkins). Private insurance runs $100–$300/month. Medical procedures cost 40–60% less than the US. Dental and prescription drugs are a fraction of US prices.
JCI-Accredited Hospitals: Yes

📋 Retirement Visa (O-A)
Thailand's Non-Immigrant O-A visa is specifically for retirees aged 50+. Options: prove 800,000 Thai Baht (~$24,000) in a Thai bank account, OR show $2,400/year income, OR a combination. Annual renewal required. No work permitted. Chiang Mai, Bangkok, Phuket, and Koh Samui are the most popular areas.
Age Requirement: 50+
🏥 Healthcare
Thailand has some of the world's best private hospitals at 60–80% less than US prices. Bumrungrad International (Bangkok) and Bangkok Hospital are JCI-accredited and attract medical tourists globally. Private health insurance runs $100–$250/month. Many expats fly home occasionally for specialized care.
Medical Tourism Leader: Yes
💰 Taxes & Banking
Thailand does not tax foreign income not brought into the country in the same tax year. New 2024 rules may change this for income remitted to Thailand — consult an expat tax advisor. No US-Thailand tax treaty. You still file and pay US taxes. Banking in Thailand is accessible for retirees with a valid visa.
Tax Rule Changes: 2024 Update

📋 Non-Lucrative Visa
Spain's Non-Lucrative Residency Visa requires proof of ~€28,000/year in passive income (about $2,400/mo). No work permitted. Valid for 1 year, renewable. After 5 years: permanent residency. After 10: citizenship eligible. Popular regions: Costa del Sol, Alicante, Barcelona, Madrid, Canary Islands.
Income Requirement: Higher
🏥 Healthcare
Spain has one of the world's top-ranked healthcare systems. Legal residents can access the public system (SNS). Private insurance is required for the visa application — runs €80–€200/month. Major cities have English-speaking private hospitals of excellent quality.
Healthcare Ranking: Top 10 Global
💰 Taxes — Beckham Law
The Beckham Law (IRPF Special Regime) allows new residents to pay a flat 24% tax on Spain-sourced income for up to 5 years. US-Spain tax treaty exists. US Social Security is taxed in the US only under the treaty. Consult an expat CPA — tax situation is complex for US-Spain retirees.
Tax Treaty: Yes / Complex

Retirement Safety Center

Protect Your Money, Identity & Retirement Savings

Protecting your retirement income is as important as growing it. Fraud targeting older Americans is rising every year. Use these resources to recognize scams, protect your identity, and report fraud.

🚨 FTC Data: Americans reported losing $10 billion to fraud in 2023 — the highest amount on record. Adults over 60 are the most targeted group. The most common attack vectors: phone impersonation of government agencies, online investment opportunities, and romance-based financial requests. Knowing the warning signs is your best protection.
⚠️
Top Scams Targeting Retirees
An overview of the most common fraud categories affecting older Americans today, with current loss statistics from the FTC.
💔
Romance & Online Relationship Scams
Fraudsters build emotional relationships over weeks or months before making financial requests. Learn how to identify and stop this type of fraud.
💼
Employment & Work-From-Home Scams
Fake job offers targeting retirees seeking supplemental income, including reshipping scams, advance-fee schemes, and fraudulent work-from-home positions.
📈
Investment & Crypto Scams
Fraudulent investment opportunities, guaranteed-return schemes, and cryptocurrency fraud that target retirement savings specifically.
🏥
Medical & Medicare Fraud
Scammers impersonating Medicare representatives to steal your Medicare ID number, bill for services never received, and commit identity theft.
📖
Self-Publishing & Vanity Press Scams
Companies charging thousands of dollars for publishing services that reputable publishers provide for free, targeting retirees who want to write their life story.

🚩 Red Flag Library

Source: Federal Trade Commission consumer fraud guidance. Any single red flag is a reason to stop and independently verify before proceeding.

Financial Warning Signs
Unexpected investment opportunity with guaranteed returns
Pressure to act immediately — limited-time offer
Requests for payment by gift card, wire transfer, or cryptocurrency
Upfront fee required to receive a prize, inheritance, or loan
Returns that sound too good to be true
Identity & Technology Warning Signs
Unknown tech support calls claiming your computer has a virus
Request to install remote access software on your device
Email or text with a link asking you to verify your account
Caller claiming to be from a government agency by phone
Request for Social Security number or Medicare ID by phone
Relationship & Social Warning Signs
Romance contacts asking for money — even small amounts
New online contact introducing an investment opportunity
Anyone asking you to keep a financial matter secret from family
Job offer requiring upfront payment for training or supplies
Publisher or agent requiring large fees before representing your work

🖥 Technology Protection Guide

Major technology companies now include built-in fraud protection tools. Here is what is available and how to use each one to protect yourself.

Microsoft
Windows Security & Scam-Blocking
Windows Defender includes real-time phishing protection and scam website blocking. Enable it via Windows Security → App & Browser Control → Reputation-based protection. Microsoft also provides a free call-blocking feature through the Phone Link app.
Apple
Call Screening & Silence Unknown Callers
iPhone users can enable Silence Unknown Callers under Settings → Phone. This automatically sends calls from numbers not in your contacts to voicemail. iOS also warns when you visit known scam websites in Safari.
Mastercard
Fraud Intelligence & Zero Liability
Mastercard monitors transactions in real time for unusual patterns and notifies cardholders of suspicious activity. All Mastercard holders are protected by Zero Liability — you are not responsible for unauthorized transactions you report promptly.
McAfee
Scam Detection & Identity Monitoring
McAfee Scam Protection uses AI to detect and block scam links in text messages, emails, and social media before you click them. McAfee Identity Monitoring alerts you if your personal information appears in data breaches or on the dark web.

📋 How to Report a Scam

Reporting fraud helps authorities track trends, warn other retirees, and in some cases recover losses. Use the appropriate channel based on the type of fraud you experienced.

Federal Agency Federal Trade Commission Report any consumer fraud, scam phone calls, fake websites, identity theft, or deceptive business practices. reportfraud.ftc.gov Social Security Administration SSA Office of Inspector General Report impersonation of SSA employees, Social Security number misuse, and benefit fraud. oig.ssa.gov/report Medicare / Health & Human Services HHS Office of Inspector General Report Medicare fraud, fraudulent billing, and healthcare identity theft. oig.hhs.gov/fraud Federal Bureau of Investigation FBI Internet Crime Complaint Center Report internet-based fraud, online investment scams, email phishing, and large financial losses. ic3.gov State Government State Attorney General Each state has a consumer protection division. Report local scams, fraudulent businesses, and elder financial abuse to your state Attorney General. naag.org — Find Your AG Consumer Finance Consumer Financial Protection Bureau Report financial product fraud, predatory lending, deceptive bank practices, and investment scams involving financial institutions. consumerfinance.gov/complaint
